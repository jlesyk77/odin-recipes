The following is a breif description about how to make a delicious carnitas birria!

You can use your carnitas birria to make anything you please with it, but, personally I think they're best as quesatacos!

You will first need a nice pork shoulder. I recommend at least a 3lb shoulder. (* As a side note: pork butt and pork shoulder as the same exact piece of meat so either will do!)

Make sure to unwrap your shoulder from the butcher's twine and cover with mustard. Be generous enough to coat the entire shoulder, but, it doesn't need more than a thin-medium coating. This will only be used as a binder for your spice rub.

Then add your spice dry rub. This can be anything you want, but for pork I highly recommend something that is heavy with a sugary-sweet element, something that has a lot of spice to balance the sweetness, and onion powder seems to be a great flavor to round out your spice rub. Of course you should also be using a mandatory of the 3 most basic ingredients (salt, pepper, and garlic powder).

After this follow the short list of steps below to cook yourself delicioius carnitas birria!

  1. Let the spice rubbed shoulder marinate for a minimum of 2 hours, up to 24 hours in the fridge.
  2. Take out the shoulder and place in slow cooker on low.
  3. Add 1-2 onions, quarted, a few cloves of garlic, and any other vegetables and herbs that you would like to flavor your shoulder with.
  4. Then add equal parts hot sauce and Coca-cola, making sure to only fill the cooker up 1-1.5 inches. The pork fat will render down and add to this braising liquid.
  5. Let cook on low heat for a minimum of 6 hours, up to 10-12 depending on size.
  6. After it's cook time, and the pork is beginning to shred effortlessly, turn your slow cooker off and unplug it.
  7. Allow the meat to rest in the covered slow cooker for a further 2-12 hours, depending on your personal preference. I like to time things so that the cook time ends as I'm going to sleep, and by the morning I wake up to the juiciest and most tender carnitas birria you've ever tasted.
